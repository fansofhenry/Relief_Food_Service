Dear Hiring Committee,

I grew up in Palo Alto. I went to Gunn High School — not far from your campus. Lucile Packard Children's Hospital has been part of the fabric of this community my entire life: a place where families come during the hardest and most vulnerable moments they will ever face. Wanting to be part of the team that supports those families, even in a small way, is not something I arrived at casually.

For four years, I worked at In-N-Out Burger in Mountain View. That might sound like an unlikely foundation for a children's hospital application — but In-N-Out runs one of the most rigorous food safety and quality programs in the industry. Every shift, I prepared food to strict protocols, portioned items with precision, operated commercial kitchen equipment, managed stock rotation, and made sure serving areas were always clean, stocked, and ready. I trained incoming associates. I didn't miss shifts. I learned, in a very real way, that in a kitchen, consistency is care.

"On a patient floor, someone's meal isn't just a preference — it's part of their treatment. I've spent four years treating every order as a responsibility. I'm ready to bring that exact mindset to patient trays at LPCH."

More recently, as a Program Coordinator at Foothill and Skyline Colleges, I've been running high-volume, high-stakes operations for diverse student populations — training staff, maintaining strict compliance documentation (financial aid records aren't so different from HACCP logs: real people depend on their accuracy), and managing the kind of procedural detail that has genuine consequences when it slips. That institutional discipline transfers directly.

My academic background — a B.S. in Computer Science & Humanities from San Jose State, with a thesis on using technology for social good, and an A.S. in Computer Science & Learning Science from Foothill — might seem unusual for a food service role. What it gives me, practically, is an instinct for understanding why a system works the way it does and then following it correctly because I grasp the reasoning, not just the rule. That matters in a regulated healthcare environment.

I'm local. I'm reliable. I genuinely care about the community LPCH serves — it's my community too. And I believe that a warm, accurate, carefully prepared meal delivered on time to a child in a hospital bed is not a small thing. I'd be honored to be part of making that happen.

With sincere gratitude,

Henry Fan
